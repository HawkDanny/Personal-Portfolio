The Death of Mr. Montague

A role playing card game with discrete life events as improvisational cannon fodder

The Game

The Death of Mr. Montague is an analog game designed to foster an environment of bickering, lying, and self-aggrandizing. After the death of an intriguing upscale gentleman by the name of Maximus Montague, all who knew him in life gather to claim his inheritance. Calling upon even the most tenuous of connections to Montague, a silver tongued player finds themselves at an advantage when it is revealed that Montague's will mentions no names, but merely characteristics.

There are 3 types of cards:

Character Card

Who you are, how you know Mr. Montague

Will Card

A piece of wealth and who it is allotted to

Event Card

Use to establish your shared history with Montague

Montague's Journal

With records of all major life events. Names redacted

A narrative is only as strong as the possibility space it creates. Don't shrink the possibility space.


Leave room for narrative extrapolation. Give the player enough breadcrumbs, and eventually they'll figure out something on their own.


Montague presents its narrative as discrete nodes, rooted in a timeline. The players weave these nodes together, and create a unique story each playthrough.
