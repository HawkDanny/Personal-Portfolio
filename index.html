    IMAGINE

    2019

    Game

    Game?

    Unity

    Tiny

    In this game I tell you to imagine spring, because I can't imagine it for you. So please just close your eyes and give it a go. WASD and mouse to control. Created in ten hours for the Game Center's Prototype Studio class, as part of my MFA in Game Design.

    LIT.ROCKS

    2018

    Art

    Shaders

    Unity

    Instagram

    In 2018 I was really insecure in my ability to do any kind of visual or look development in Unity, and I decided to change that by doing daily exercises. For each post I had two constraints — there had to be a shader involved and I had to use a low poly rock model from an asset pack. I also made some (not all) of the music used.

    DANNY HAWK'S BOOP GAME

    2018

    Game

    Arduino

    Arcade Controls

    Booping

    For a while I wanted to make a game where competitive players have to share a control. That started as a web prototype, but it didn’t work quite as intended so I made a custom arcade controller for it. Exhibited at Imagine RIT 2018.

    BEAUTIFUL SERIES

    2018

    Art

    Physical Computing

    Arduino

    Beauty

    In 2018 I was experimenting with physical computing because my eyes were getting tired of looking at computers all day. The Beautiful Series is a series of pieces I made that all revolved around beauty. Each piece is intended to subvert good/expected design.

    NARWHAL PICNIC

    2017

    Game

    VR

    Unity

    Narwhals

    The coolest thing about VR is that the user is blind to the world around them, so I made Narwhal Picnic as an exploration of that design space, with an emphasis on player expression within a scene. It was super successful at both creating a very playful experience and also leaving red rings on my friends' foreheads.

    MY DOG'S EYES

    2017

    Art

    Creative Coding

    HTML5 Canvas

    Zammuto

    The artist Zammuto wrote a song titled "My Dog's Eyes" and the first time I heard it I stood still. It is my favorite song. The words are steeped in imagery, and I used the words themselves to construct the moments they describe. This piece is my lover letter to the song.
